IU FACULTY MENTORS

Below is a list of IUI faculty mentors for the Datawiz-IN Scholars Program. The updated 2025 faculty mentors and their research description will be published soon. Please check back regularly. Thank you for your patience.

Lata Balakrishnan, Ph.D.

Dr. Lata Balakrishnan is an Associate Professor of Biology. Her research focuses on understanding the mechanistic reactions of eukaryotic lagging-strand DNA synthesis and the accompanying DNA repair processes. Her lab integrates three powerful tools—biochemistry, chromatin biology, and genetics—to study DNA replication and repair.

Nicholas Berbari, Ph.D.

Dr. Nicholas Berbari is an Associate Professor of Biology and Neuroscience. His research focuses on the roles of small, hair-like cellular appendages called cilia, particularly in brain function. He is currently investigating how cilia influence appetite and satiety using conditional and congenital knockout mouse models, tissue culture, pharmacology, and electrophysiology.

Shaun J. Grannis, M.D.

Dr. Shaun Grannis is a Professor of Medical Informatics at the Regenstrief Institute and a Professor of Family Medicine. He also serves as an Adjunct Professor at the School of Public Health and the School of Informatics and Computing. His research focuses on improving discovery and decision support by developing, testing, and implementing innovative approaches for data integration, patient matching, predictive modeling, and other novel data science applications. His work includes developing population health data frameworks that integrate community and social determinants of health with clinical data, as well as leveraging machine learning-based models to enhance discovery and decision support in various contexts.

Kun Huang, Ph.D.

Kun Huang is the Chair of the Department of Biostatistics and Health Data Science. He is a professor of Medicine. Also, he works as an adjunct professor of Epidemiology, School of Public Health and at School of Informatics and Computing, School of Computer Science. In addition to this, he is titled Associate Director for Data Science, IU Simon Comprehensive Cancer Center.

His research interests lie in translational bioinformatics, computational pathology, medical imaging, computational biology, integrative genomics tools for precision health, computer vision, and machine learning. He is focused on translating informatics tools to improve clinical practice and accelerate scientific discovery and believes the Precision Health Initiative is an unprecedented opportunity to accomplish just that.

Sarath C. Janga, Ph.D.

Dr. Sarath Chandra Janga leads a team of researchers who seek to identify molecular players at different scales that interfere with the normal functioning of cellular networks in humans and other model organisms. Professor Janga’s research integrates computational and experimental multi-omic approaches to study complex and heterogeneous biomedical data, with the goal of understanding how the regulation, structure, and dynamics of biological systems shape the phenotypic landscape of an organism and its relevance to disease conditions.

His interdisciplinary research team, in collaboration with clinical and translational scientists, examines these questions using cutting-edge next-generation sequencing technologies and data science frameworks. They develop novel bioinformatics algorithms to understand how RNA interactions, structures, and their cross-talk with various cellular entities in cell lines and animal models can be employed for dissecting complex phenotypes in the liver, brain, kidney, and immune diseases.

Babar A. Khan, MD

Babar A. Khan is an Associate Professor of Medicine and Research Scientist at Regenstrief Institute Inc. and Indiana University Center for Aging Research. He is a patient-oriented translational/clinical researcher with a principal focus on developing a biomarker profile among delirious patients in the intensive care unit to predict their long-term cognitive, physical, and psychological morbidity.

In addition, he directs the Critical Care Recovery Center – CCRC, located at Eskenazi Health Services.

Bohdan Khomtchouk, Ph.D.

Dr. Bohdan Khomtchouk is an Assistant Professor of Bioinformatics and Data Science in the Department of BioHealth Informatics at Indiana University’s Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering in Indianapolis. His research group is pioneering the field of cardioinformatics, working at the intersection of bioinformatics and precision cardiology. His team integrates computational biology and AI/machine learning-based approaches to advance cardiovascular, renal, and metabolic biology while enabling the discovery of new therapeutics.

Yunlong Liu, Ph.D.

Dr. Yunlong Liu is the Director of the Center for Computational Biology & Bioinformatics and the Center for Medical Genomics. He is a Professor of Medical & Molecular Genetics and Biostatistics, as well as an Adjunct Professor of Medicine and the School of Informatics and Computing. His lab, the Laboratory for Computational Genomics, employs systems biology approaches to investigate gene expression regulation, including transcriptional, post-transcriptional, and epigenetic mechanisms.

James A. Marrs, Ph.D.

Dr. James A. Marrs is a Professor and the Director of Graduate Studies. His research focuses on cell biology, developmental biology, and developmental neurobiology. His work uses zebrafish as a model organism to study fetal alcohol syndrome, aiming to identify critical genes involved in birth defects caused by ethanol exposure.

Samy Meroueh, Ph.D.

Dr. Samy Meroueh is a Professor of Biochemistry & Molecular Biology. His research focuses on using three-dimensional protein structures to design small molecules that modulate protein function. His current work involves targeting the urokinase-type plasminogen activator system, serine/threonine kinases, aldehyde dehydrogenases, and voltage-gated calcium channels.

Andrew D. Miller, Ph.D.

Andrew D. Miller is an Assistant Professor of Human-Computer Interaction. He studies how social computing technology can empower people to help each other with their health and wellness. His research areas of interest include social computing technology, social support for health and wellness, pervasive technologies, and connecting in-patient, clinical, home, and school environments.

Miller is a member of the ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems.

Amber L. Mosley, Ph.D.

Dr. Amber L. Mosley is a Professor of Biochemistry & Molecular Biology and Medical & Molecular Genetics. Her research focuses on the regulation of RNA Polymerase II elongation and termination using systems-level approaches, including next-generation sequencing and protein mass spectrometry.

Kwangsik Nho, Ph.D.

Dr. Kwangsik Nho is an Associate Professor of Radiology and Imaging Sciences and an Adjunct Associate Professor at the School of Informatics and Computing. His research expertise includes bioinformatics, imaging informatics, and medical informatics. He is particularly interested in integrating bioinformatics, medical informatics, and imaging informatics methodologies to investigate the genetic etiology of complex diseases such as Alzheimer's disease.

Christine J. Picard, Ph.D.

Dr. Christine J. Picard is the Associate Dean for Research and Graduate Education at the IU Indianapolis School of Science and a Professor in the Department of Biology. Her lab focuses on the population genetics and genomics of forensically important insects, utilizing high-throughput sequencing to study genetic control and natural variation in carrion insect phenotypes.

Contact: cpicard@iu.edu | Research Lab Website

Saptarshi Purkayastha, Ph.D.

Saptarshi Purkayastha is a tenured Associate Professor of Data Science and Health Informatics at Indiana University Indianapolis. He is the Program Director of the MS in Health Informatics at the Department of Biomedical Engineering and Informatics. His research focuses on discipline-based education, health information systems, and biomedical data analytics.

His expertise spans teaching-learning infrastructures, electronic health record (EHR) systems, mobile health information systems, and health information interoperability. He integrates process-oriented guided inquiry learning (POGIL) with active learning strategies in multidisciplinary biomedical informatics courses, engaging students from medicine, nursing, computer science, business, law, and pharmacy.

Dr. Purkayastha has played a pivotal role in implementing National Health Information Systems (HIS) in multiple countries, including Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, and North Korea, under the auspices of the World Health Organization. His projects extend to telemedicine, CAD applications, mHealth, real-time decision-making in robotics, distributed video encoding, and AI-driven educational technology for individuals with dyslexia.

At IUI, he teaches courses on Biomedical Data Analytics, Database Design for Health, EHR Systems, Health Information Exchange, and Clinical Decision Support Systems. His research continues to bridge computing, healthcare, and education through innovative methods in data-driven healthcare applications.

Sara K. Quinney, Ph.D.

Dr. Sara K. Quinney is an Associate Professor of Obstetrics & Gynecology, an Associate Professor of Medicine, and an Adjunct Associate Professor of Health Informatics at the School of Informatics and Computing. She also serves as the Director of the Disease and Therapeutic Response Modeling Program. Her research spans pharmacometric and big data applications, including physiologically based pharmacokinetic modeling, text mining, and machine learning approaches. She is involved in several collaborative research projects, including the MODEL-AD preclinical trial core, as well as studies in oncology and opioid use disorder.

Khairi Reda, Ph.D.

Dr. Khairi Reda is an Assistant Professor of Data Science and Human-Computer Interaction. His research focuses on designing, developing, and evaluating perceptual data interfaces to help people interact with data more effectively. He investigates the technological and human factors that influence data consumption and decision-making. Dr. Reda is particularly interested in the application of data visualization to catalyze scientific discovery and collaborates with experts across disciplines to develop new visualization techniques for scientific data.

Randall Roper, Ph.D.

Dr. Randall Roper is an Associate Professor of Biology and an Adjunct Associate Professor of Biomedical Engineering. His research focuses on understanding the genetic and developmental bases of phenotypes associated with Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome). Using mouse models of Down syndrome, his work provided the first experimental evidence that trisomy adversely affects neural crest cells, which are precursors to the craniofacial skeleton. He is currently involved in preclinical studies testing treatments for specific Down syndrome traits by targeting overexpressed gene products caused by trisomy.

Titus Schleyer, Ph.D., D.M.D.

Dr. Titus Schleyer is a Professor of Medicine. He also works as Clem McDonald Professor of Biomedical Informatics. Dr. Titus Schleyer has been active in biomedical informatics research since 1989, conducting seminal research on electronic dental records and Internet applications, and workflow and human-computer interaction in dentistry.

Thankam Thyvalikakath, D.M.D, M.D.S, B.D.S, Ph.D, M.S.

Thankam Thyvalikakath is a Professor at the Indiana University School of Dentistry and an Associate Dean for Dental Informatics and Digital Health. Dr. Thyvalikakath’s research focuses on leveraging electronic health record data for clinical research and quality improvement purposes; applying user-centered design and cognitive engineering methods to design clinical systems that better support clinicians’ decision-making processes; implementation and evaluation of clinical decision support systems, and facilitating health information exchange to promote coordinated care between medical and dental care providers.

Brian Walker, Ph.D.

Dr. Brian Walker is the Daniel and Lori Efroymson Professor of Oncology, a Professor of Medicine, a Professor of Medical & Molecular Genetics, and an Adjunct Professor of BioHealth Informatics. His research focuses on the genomics and epigenetics of multiple myeloma (MM) and related plasma cell dyscrasias. He investigates the structure of the myeloma genome, from single-nucleotide mutations to large structural events such as translocations, and how these genetic alterations impact patient prognoses.

Juexin Wang, Ph.D.

Juexin Wang is Assistant Professor of Bioinformatics at the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering in Indianapolis. Dr. Wang has a long-standing interest in studying machine learning algorithms, tool development, and data analysis in bioinformatics. His academic training and research experience have provided him with an excellent background in multiple disciplines, including computer science, statistics, and computational biology. He is currently developing methods to model single-cell and spatial transcriptomics data, and data analysis in kidney diseases, cancer, and nervous system diseases. Some presentative works are published in Nature Communications and Bioinformatics.

Selena Wang, Ph.D.

Dr. Selena Wang is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Biostatistics and Health Data Science, Indiana University School of Medicine. Her lab focuses on applying and developing scalable statistical and computational approaches for high-dimensional biomedical data with emphases on network science, high-dimensional data analysis, neuroimaging, data integration, and statistical machine learning. Students will have the opportunity to contribute to both substantive neuroscience research and method innovations.

Jingwen Yan, Ph.D.

Jingwen Yan is an Assistant professor of Bioinformatics. Jingwen Yan’s work is focused on integrating data sources to extract knowledge that can lead to better understanding of complex diseases, neurological conditions, and dementias, such as Alzheimer’s disease. Her research focuses on exploring genotype-phenotype associations by developing efficient and scalable computational and bioinformatics approaches. Specifically, Yan is interested in the combination of machine learning and network science such that rich biological knowledge can be properly incorporated to guide learning.

Chi Zhang, Ph.D.

Chi Zhang is an Assistant Professor of Medical & Molecular Genetics. His interests are centered around independent research in computational modeling of disease microenvironment, cell type-specific activity and transcriptional regulation, and computational algorithm development, and collaborative research in the identification of biological characteristics related to disease progression and drug resistance.

Jie Zhang, Ph.D.

Jie Zhang is an Assistant Professor of Medical & Molecular Genetics, Adjunct Assistant Professor of Biostatistics & Health Data Science, Adjunct Assistant Professor, School of Informatics & Computing and Assistant Professor in Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics. Her research is focused on developing and applying translational bioinformatics and system biology methods to identify disease genes, pathways, and biomarkers with applications in cancers, neurological diseases, and other types of diseases.
